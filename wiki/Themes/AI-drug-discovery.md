# AI Drug Discovery

## Overview
Companies applying machine learning, automated biology, and generative chemistry to replace or accelerate traditional drug discovery. The defining characteristic is a proprietary biological or chemical dataset that compounds in value with each experiment — creating a data moat that is difficult to replicate. The thesis: AI-native drug finders will become infrastructure for large pharma, monetizing via partnerships, milestones, and royalties while also building their own clinical pipelines.

## Why Interesting
Traditional drug discovery takes 10–15 years and $2B+ per approved drug; AI platforms are compressing this by an order of magnitude. The partnership model (sell the platform to Lilly, Sanofi, Roche, Genentech) generates non-dilutive milestones and royalty tails while in-house programs create direct equity in outcomes. Companies with proprietary biological datasets (not just ML models on public data) have the most defensible moats. The Eli Lilly $2.75B Recursion collaboration (March 2026) is the most visible validation of this model to date.

## Key Trends
- **Big pharma validation**: Lilly $2.75B (RXRX, March 2026), Sanofi $1.3B Recursion deal, Roche/Genentech + Bayer partnerships. Large pharma is outsourcing AI discovery rather than building it in-house — confirms platform model.
- **End-to-end stacks forming**: Recursion + Exscientia merger created first public company that covers phenomics → target ID → generative chemistry → clinical candidate in one platform.
- **Founder transitions are a risk**: RXRX CEO Gibson stepped back Jan 2026 (to Chairman). Founder departure history correlates with execution risk in platform biotechs.
- **Insider selling is a systemic flag**: Across AI drug discovery names, insiders are net sellers despite bullish partnership narratives. Requires explanation before a high-conviction position.

## Key Company Signals
