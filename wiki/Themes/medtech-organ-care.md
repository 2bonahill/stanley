# MedTech — Organ Care

## Overview
The organ transplant market is gated by organ availability, not demand. Millions of patients need transplants; the bottleneck is viable donor organs. Organ Care technology physically expands the supply of usable organs by preserving them outside the body in warm, perfused, metabolizing states — rather than cold ischemic storage (ice). This is a platform play on expanding a market that cannot otherwise grow.

## Why Interesting
- Captive recurring revenue: every procedure requires proprietary consumables
- Platform optionality: heart → lung → liver → kidney, each a separate commercial program
- Regulatory moat: FDA approval per indication; high barriers to entry
- Founder-led science: surgeons and engineers who understand the biology deeply
- Aligned with precision medicine trends — better organ matching = better outcomes

## Key Trends
- UNOS policy changes increasing use of DCD (donation after cardiac death) donors — expands the pool of marginal organs where OCS has the greatest advantage over cold storage
- ENHANCE trial (TMDX) will be the largest and most rigorous heart preservation RCT ever run — a positive readout would set a new standard of care
- Reimbursement expansion: payers increasingly approving NOP (National OCS Program) logistics costs as outcomes improve
- Aviation-integrated organ logistics emerging as a moat — TMDX now operates 22 aircraft
- International expansion: TMDX Mercedes-Benz Italy partnership (Sep 2025) extends the NOP logistics model to ground transport in Europe — validates international replication potential
- Stock has retreated 24% from March highs ($149 → $113) while analyst consensus remains Buy at $156.60 avg target — potential re-entry window
