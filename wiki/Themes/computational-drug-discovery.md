# Computational Drug Discovery

## Overview
Companies that use physics-based simulation, AI/ML, and large biological datasets to dramatically accelerate and de-risk drug discovery. Unlike pure AI drug discovery (black-box), physics-based platforms like Schrödinger's generate predictions with mechanistic interpretability. These platforms create two revenue streams: software licensing (recurring, high-margin) plus an internal drug pipeline built at a fraction of traditional pharma cost.

## Why Interesting
- Software-first economics: SaaS moat provides revenue floor regardless of pipeline outcomes
- AI-accelerated hit-to-lead generates drug candidates in months vs. years — compresses the value creation timeline
- Platform customers (top-10 pharma) are sticky; switching costs are high once workflows are embedded
- Physics-based FEP+ is the gold standard for binding affinity prediction — used by 31/32 top pharma companies
- Drug pipeline built at $1-5M/candidate vs. $50-100M for traditional pharma — optionality at low cost
