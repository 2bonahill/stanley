# Biotech — Aging Biology

## Overview
Companies using the biology of human aging as a drug discovery engine. Rather than targeting individual diseases, these firms identify molecular hallmarks of biological aging — inflammaging, cellular senescence, NAD+ depletion, mTOR dysregulation — and develop therapeutics that slow or reverse these processes, treating multiple age-related diseases from a single mechanistic target.

## Why Interesting
- Aging is the largest single risk factor across metabolic disease, cardiovascular disease, neurodegeneration, and cancer — a single platform can address multiple $10B+ markets
- Computational/genomic approaches to target selection (using longevity data from exceptional agers) provide differentiated target identification vs. traditional hypothesis-driven pharma
- NLRP3 inhibitors are an emerging drug class with no approved therapies — first-mover advantage in a novel mechanism
- Small molecule / oral formulation preference increases commercial potential vs. biologics
