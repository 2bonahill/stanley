# Quantum Computing

## Overview
Quantum computing uses quantum mechanical phenomena (superposition, entanglement) to perform computations impossible or impractical for classical computers. The sector is transitioning from research-stage to early commercial deployment, with hardware, software, and networking all converging. Key hardware approaches: trapped-ion (IonQ), superconducting (IBM, Google), photonic, neutral atom. The race is toward fault-tolerant, error-corrected quantum computers capable of practical advantage in drug discovery, materials science, cryptography, and optimization.

## Why Interesting
- Deep technology with genuine scientific moat — years of PhD-level research required to compete
- Platform play: hardware + cloud access + software + networking creates durable ecosystems
- Government/defense spending accelerating (Air Force, NSA, DoE all active)
- 2025–2026 marks first real commercial revenue at scale for leading players
- Quantum networking and quantum security are emerging sub-sectors
