# Drone Autonomy & Defense

## Overview
The US military and government are undergoing a rapid shift to autonomous drone platforms for surveillance, combat, border security, and logistics. The FCC's restriction on foreign (primarily DJI/Chinese) drones from US government use is creating a forced migration to domestically manufactured, US-controlled drone stacks. This creates durable demand for companies across hardware, communications, AI/software, and counter-drone layers.

## Why Interesting
- Structural regulatory tailwind: FCC foreign drone restrictions = mandatory US stack adoption
- Defense budgets are growing; drone programs are a priority across DoD and DHS
- Multiple layers to the stack — communications, autonomy software, hardware, counter-drone — each potentially investable
- Recurring government contracts with multi-year lock-in
- Combat experience from Ukraine war is accelerating US urgency on autonomous systems
