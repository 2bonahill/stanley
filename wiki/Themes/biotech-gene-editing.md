# Theme: Next-Gen Gene Editing
> **Focus:** The transition from "cut-and-random-repair" (CRISPR-Cas9) to "search-and-replace" (Prime) and "single-letter" rewriting (Base Editing).

---

## 🚀 Thesis
The first wave of gene editing (CRISPR-Cas9) proved the technology works but was limited by double-strand breaks and insertion/deletion (indel) risk. Next-gen editing (Base and Prime) enables precise corrections without cutting the DNA backbone, unlocking much larger addressable markets in liver, muscle, and neurological diseases.

## 🏗️ The Stack
1.  **The Pencil (Base Editing):** Rewrites single letters (C to T, A to G). Leader: [BEAM](../Companies/BEAM.md).
2.  **The Word Processor (Prime Editing):** Search-and-replace for almost any mutation. Leader: [PRME](../Companies/PRME.md).
3.  **The Off-the-Shelf (Allogeneic):** CRISPR-edited cell therapies for cancer. Leader: [CRBU](../Companies/CRBU.md).
4.  **The In Vivo Leader:** Traditional CRISPR delivered directly into the body. Leader: [NTLA](../Companies/NTLA.md).
