# Advanced Air Mobility (Electric Aviation)

## Overview
Companies developing electric aircraft, eVTOL vehicles, and associated infrastructure for next-generation aviation. Focus is on founder-led companies with proprietary hardware, FAA certification progress, and real customer deployments — not vaporware.

## Why Interesting
Electric aviation is at the same inflection point as electric vehicles in 2015–2017. The FAA is actively building the regulatory framework (eIPP program, new certification pathways). The market spans cargo logistics (Amazon, UPS), medical (organ transport), military, and eventually passenger. Companies that control the full stack — aircraft + charging + software — have the strongest moat. The military tailwind (MV250-class drones) adds a non-cyclical revenue floor that pure commercial plays lack.

## Key Trends
- **FAA eIPP is the unlock**: DOT/FAA eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (Mar 2026) selects launch participants — BETA in 7 of 8 programs. This is the US government formally green-lighting commercial eVTOL operations. Huge regulatory de-risking.
- **H500A engine cert H1 2026**: First standalone electric aircraft engine FAA certification in the ALIA program — near-term binary catalyst.
- **Military as anchor customer**: BETA's MV250 military cargo drone accelerated 6 months (Mar 2026). DoD contracts provide non-dilutive revenue and validate the technology for commercial certification.
- **Charging infrastructure as moat**: Whoever wins the charging standard in electric aviation wins the ecosystem. BETA is building this in parallel with aircraft — analogous to Tesla's Supercharger network.
- **Post-IPO washout opportunity**: BETA IPO'd at $34 (Nov 2025), now $16.61 (-51%). $1.71B cash, no debt, 59% insider ownership. Classic post-IPO lock-up selling pressure creating entry opportunity.
- **Hawaii inter-island cargo deal (Apr 12, 2026)**: BETA reached agreement for electric aircraft operations in Hawaii — first confirmed commercial deployment outside contiguous US. Validates cargo logistics use case.
- **Lock-up expiry May 3, 2026**: 59% insider-owned; post-lock-up selling possible. Watch for dip opportunity.
