# Biotech — Metabolic Disease & GLP-1

## Overview
Companies developing novel therapies for metabolic and endocrine disorders — primarily obesity, type 2 diabetes, MASH (metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis), and related conditions. Focus is on companies with differentiated mechanisms, strong clinical data, and founder-led management outside the mega-cap GLP-1 duopoly (Novo Nordisk / Eli Lilly).

## Why Interesting
The GLP-1 obesity wave is the largest drug market opportunity in pharmaceutical history — estimated at $100B+ by 2030. The field is dominated by Novo Nordisk (semaglutide / Ozempic / Wegovy) and Eli Lilly (tirzepatide / Mounjaro / Zepbound). But the next layer — smaller biotech companies with differentiated molecules, oral formulations, or next-gen mechanisms — represents the highest upside risk/reward. Oral GLP-1 (replacing weekly injections) is the critical next frontier. MASH is now a validated, FDA-approved market (resmetirom/Rezdiffra). Companies with Phase 3-ready molecules, real clinical data, and founder-led management represent compelling small-cap exposure before large-cap M&A or partnering events.

## Key Trends
- **Oral GLP-1 is the game-changer**: Weekly injections are the current standard (Wegovy, Zepbound). Oral formulation is the mass-market unlock — 10x larger addressable population. Viking's oral VK2735 Phase 2 data (12.2% weight loss at 13 weeks) is the best published oral GLP-1 Phase 2 result. Phase 3 initiation target Q3 2026.
- **Phase 3 enrollment = de-risking milestone**: Both VANQUISH-1 (Nov 2025) and VANQUISH-2 (Mar 2026) are now fully enrolled. ~2,000 total patients in 78-week treatment. Data readout expected ~2027. Enrollment risk is now behind us.
- **MASH is a validated market**: Resmetirom (Rezdiffra) approved 2024. VK2809 (TRβ agonist, Phase 2b VOYAGE completed) positions Viking as a MASH optionality play with a partnering asset.
- **CEO selling is a persistent flag**: Viking's CEO sold $7.7M of stock on Jan 5, 2026. CFO sold same day. For a company with no revenue, this is a material negative signal to watch.
- **M&A backdrop**: Novo, Lilly, Pfizer, Roche are all actively acquiring GLP-1/obesity assets. Viking at $3.97B cap could be acquisition target if Phase 3 data are strong.
- **GPCR added 2026-04-11**: Structure Therapeutics (GPCR) graduated from opportunities to watchlist. Aleniglipron Phase 2 ACCESS II: 16.3% weight loss — highest ever for oral GLP-1. Phase 3 H2 2026. FDA Type B meeting Q2 2026. Score 13/15.
