# Biotech — Protein Degradation

## Overview
Companies developing targeted protein degradation (TPD) therapies — small molecules that eliminate disease-causing proteins using the cell's own proteasome, rather than merely blocking their activity. The defining advantage over conventional drugs: degraders can target transcription factors and other "undruggable" proteins, operate catalytically (sub-stoichiometric doses), and achieve complete pathway suppression. Oral administration (unlike most biologics) further expands patient access. TPD is an early-stage but rapidly validating modality — Kymera's Phase 1b data (Dec 2025) is the most clinically advanced public readout in the space.

## Why Interesting
Most large-disease-area proteins (transcription factors, scaffolding proteins, nuclear receptors) have no druggable active site — they've been resistant to small-molecule inhibition for decades. Degradation sidesteps this entirely by redirecting the cell's own protein disposal system. The catalytic mechanism means one degrader molecule can destroy many protein copies — superior pharmacology. The first PROTAC degraders (ARV-110, ARV-471 from Arvinas) have now shown Phase 2/3 clinical activity, validating the biology at scale. Kymera is applying this to immunology — a broader market than oncology and with STAT6 as the clearest near-term target.

## Key Trends
- **STAT6 degradation validated in humans**: KT-621 Phase 1b (Dec 2025) is the first clinical confirmation that STAT6 can be depleted 94–98% systemically via an oral degrader. This is a mechanism-validation milestone, not just dose-finding.
- **Oral vs. biologic**: KT-621 vs. dupilumab comparison will be the key Phase 2b read. If oral STAT6 degradation matches biologic STAT6 blocking, the market for oral AD/asthma treatment is multiples larger than injectable.
- **Platform licensing**: Sanofi and Gilead already paying milestones for TPD programs — validates that big pharma views the Pegasus platform as infrastructure, not just a drug candidate.
- **Arvinas Phase 3 as sector signal**: ARV-471 in breast cancer is the leading public TPD program in oncology; positive data would validate the modality broadly and likely re-rate KYMR.
