## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Build and own the full vertical stack — from semiconductor lasers through finished transceivers — that moves data inside and between AI data centers at the lowest power and highest speed.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
Dr. Chih-Hsiang (Thompson) Lin, Ph.D. (Electrical Engineering, University of Missouri) founded AAOI in 1997, serves as Chairman, President, and CEO, and has held that role continuously since inception. He has more than 10 U.S. patents and 200+ technical papers. The mission is personal — he built the company from a university research lab and never sold it. Execution risk: Lin is a scientist-operator; commercial scaling and customer diversification beyond three hyperscalers are the gaps an engineer-founder struggles most with.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
- **Optical transceivers (400G / 800G / 1.6T LPO):** Modules that convert electrical signals into light pulses for data center inter-rack and inter-pod interconnects. LPO removes the power-hungry DSP (~50% lower power vs. DSP-based); AAOI began volume shipping 1.6T LPO in March 2026, ahead of most competitors.
- **CATV products:** 1.8 GHz amplifiers and HFC node equipment for North American cable operators — legacy cash generator, ~40% of Q4 2025 revenue.
- **Semiconductor laser chips (MBE fab):** Internal III-V laser manufacturing is the structural moat: most competitors buy lasers from AAOI or external fabs. AAOI's in-house MBE fab in Sugar Land, TX, controls the photon source that defines transceiver quality and cost.
- **Public 2026 goal:** $1 billion in revenue (vs. $456M in FY2025) — management guidance issued Feb 2026.

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- Q1 2026 earnings (May 7, 2026): First proof point toward $1B guide; GM trajectory above 31% = upside.
- 1.6T transceiver qualification at additional hyperscalers (named contract beyond current 3): would directly de-risk the concentration problem and expand addressable revenue.

**7. THESIS KILLER**
One or more of the three hyperscale customers (Amazon/Microsoft/Oracle — 91% of revenue) delays or cancels optical transceiver orders, or qualifies a competing supplier, causing revenue to miss the $1B guide by more than 25%.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
AAOI is the only publicly traded company that manufactures its own semiconductor lasers AND assembles finished 1.6T LPO transceivers at scale — a vertical integration that makes it both the lowest-cost and fastest-ramping supplier in the AI optical interconnect chain. The market is treating it as a commodity transceiver vendor when the real thesis is that the internal MBE fab is a structural cost moat that gets more valuable as speeds increase. The risk is that a $10B market cap fully prices 2026 guidance already, leaving no margin of safety if execution slips — hence bellwether status, not a priority entry.

## Technology
AAOI's structural differentiation is **in-house III-V (InP/GaAs) semiconductor laser manufacturing via Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE)** at its Sugar Land, TX fab. Building a comparable III-V fab takes 3–5 years and $200M+. Most transceiver competitors source lasers externally; AAOI controls its photon source.

**Linear Pluggable Optics (LPO):** AAOI's 800G and 1.6T transceivers eliminate the Digital Signal Processor (DSP) module, relying on high-quality internal lasers and linear drivers to maintain signal integrity. Result: ~50% lower power consumption vs. DSP-based alternatives — critical for AI training clusters running thousands of transceivers simultaneously.

**Volume 1.6T shipments began March 2026** — ahead of most large competitors in next-generation speed qualification.

**Patents:** 199 U.S. patents. Active patent enforcement: lawsuits filed against Accelight (Dec 2024), Eoptolink, and CIG for optical transceiver infringement.
