## Part 0 — Investment Case

**Mission Signal: STRONG** — Delivering practical quantum optimization value to commercial customers today — not waiting for fault-tolerant systems — is a real and differentiated mission. The QCI acquisition makes D-Wave the only dual-paradigm quantum company.

### 1. Mission
Deliver quantum advantage to commercial customers using quantum annealing for optimization today, while building gate-model capability (via QCI) for general quantum computing tomorrow.

### 2. Team & Drive
CEO Alan Baratz (joined 2020 from IBM; not a founder) has grown revenue from near-zero to $24.6M (+179% YoY). All original founders have departed. CEO Baratz has sold >$52M in stock (29 insider sells vs 1 buy) — massive insider selling on a $24.6M revenue base is the dominant negative signal. No operational founder; management conviction is near-zero by the insider buying metric.

### 7. Thesis Killer
Gate-model quantum (via QCI) fails to achieve commercial-scale applications by 2027 — leaving D-Wave's annealing as a niche optimization tool at $24M revenue against a $5–7B market cap requiring a 100x revenue ramp with no clear near-term path.

> **Compact Summary**
> **MISSION:** Quantum annealing optimization today; gate-model (QCI) for future general computation; only dual-paradigm quantum company
> **TEAM:** Non-founder CEO from IBM; ALL founders departed; CEO sold $52M+ — zero insider conviction
> **THESIS KILLER:** Gate-model fails to commercialize; annealing stays niche; $7B cap on $24.6M revenue = extreme valuation risk
