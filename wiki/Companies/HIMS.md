## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Democratize access to healthcare by building the leading telehealth platform for personalized, affordable treatments — starting with hair loss, ED, and weight loss (GLP-1), scaling to a full-stack digital pharmacy and primary care platform.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO Andrew Dudum — founder, 30 years old at IPO. Mission-driven on healthcare access. Red flag: sold $46M+ stock at $50–70 (Aug–Oct 2025) — 46 sells vs 2 buys in 5 years. Significant insider selling is the dominant negative. Company is genuinely founder-led in culture but the CEO's selling pattern undermines conviction.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
Hims & Hers is a telehealth subscription platform: patients get an online consultation, receive personalized prescriptions, and products are delivered monthly. Core verticals: hair loss, ED, mental health, skincare, and weight loss. The GLP-1 segment ($725M in 2025) was built on compounded semaglutide at $49–79/month — the margin-driving moat. Post-Novo Nordisk deal (March 9, 2026): now also distributes branded Wegovy/Ozempic at $149/month. International: $134M in 2025 (+400% YoY via Eucalyptus, Zava, Livewell acquisitions).

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Revenue: $2.35B FY2025 (+59%) → $2.75–2.9B FY2026 (+17–23% guided)
- Subscribers: 2.5M with 85% retention rate
- Gross margin: 72% FY2025 (down from 79%) — must stabilize above 68% as branded Wegovy (lower margin) replaces compounded
- GLP-1 segment: ~$725M FY2025 — watch Q1 2026 for branded-vs-compounded mix and subscriber retention

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
1. Q1 2026 earnings (~May 2026) — first data on subscriber retention at $149 branded pricing vs. prior $49 compounded. If churn is contained (<15%), the bull case remains intact
2. DOJ/SEC investigation resolution — unresolved overhang; resolution would re-rate the stock significantly
3. International expansion (Eucalyptus, Zava, Livewell) reaching $300M+ ARR — currently the most underpriced optionality

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current price: ~$21.15 (2026-04-14). 52W range: $13.74–$70.43. GREEN band: $14–18 (max risk/reward near 52W low). AMBER band: $18–24 (current zone — Tranche 1 only with Q1 confirmation).

**7. THESIS KILLER**
Q1 2026 GLP-1 subscriber count declines by >20% as patients reject $149 branded pricing after $49 compounded — and gross margin compresses below 65%, making the business model structurally less attractive than it appeared.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
HIMS trades at ~1.4x forward EV/Revenue on a $2.35B business with 85% subscriber retention, $929M cash, and the leading US telehealth distribution platform — an extreme valuation discount vs. 3–4x sector median. The market is pricing in structural failure of the GLP-1 model after the compounding/Novo saga. If Q1 2026 shows that subscribers retained at branded pricing and gross margins stabilized, this is a deeply mispriced asset. The 36–46% short float creates enormous squeeze potential on any positive data.
