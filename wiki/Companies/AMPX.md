Commercialize silicon nanowire anode lithium-ion batteries at 450–500 Wh/kg — the highest energy density of any commercially available Li-ion chemistry — targeting aerospace, defense, drone, and eVTOL markets where energy density is the binding constraint.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
Amprius makes silicon nanowire anode cells where the graphite anode is replaced by silicon nanowires — enabling 450–500 Wh/kg vs. ~250 Wh/kg for standard graphite Li-ion. Capital-light model: manufacturing outsourced to Korean foundry partners (NDAA-compliant). Key markets: aerospace (Airbus AALTO, high-altitude platforms), defense (DIU/DoD contracts), drones, and eVTOL. NDAA compliance (11/11 components) creates a 2+ year window vs. Chinese competitors ahead of Jan 2028 DoD mandate.

1. NDAA 2028 DoD mandate enforcement — US government procurement shifting to NDAA-compliant batteries by Jan 2028; Chinese battery suppliers locked out of all defense contracts
2. eVTOL certification wave (2026–2027) — every major eVTOL OEM (Joby, Archer, Wisk) needs 450+ Wh/kg batteries for certification; Amprius is one of few validated suppliers

## Technology
Silicon nanowire anode batteries. Standard Li-ion uses graphite anodes — theoretically maxed at ~372 mAh/g capacity. Amprius replaces graphite with silicon nanowires grown on a current collector, enabling ~3,500 mAh/g theoretical capacity. In practice: 450–500 Wh/kg at the cell level (vs. ~250 Wh/kg for premium graphite). The nanowire architecture addresses silicon's key failure mode (expansion/contraction cracking) through engineered geometry. Manufacturing: Korean foundry partnership (capital-light), NDAA-compliant. 50 issued patents.
