# Recursion Pharmaceuticals ($RXRX) 🟢

## Part 0 — Investment Case

**Mission Signal: STRONG** — Using AI and biological data to find cures faster and cheaper, targeting diseases that have resisted traditional drug discovery, is a clear and compelling purpose. The Eli Lilly $2.75B deal is the strongest external validation of the mission.

### 1. Mission
Decode biology at scale using AI-powered cellular data to discover drugs faster, cheaper, and with higher probability of clinical success — making the drug discovery process itself a technological advantage.

### 2. Team & Drive
Chris Gibson (PhD, co-founder) transitioned from CEO to Chairman Jan 2026; new CEO is Najat Khan (PhD, former Chief R&D Officer at J&J). Co-founder Blake Borgeson (PhD) remains VP Engineering. ALL key insiders are net sellers: Gibson $3M, Borgeson $3.4M, Khan $643K. No open-market buying confirmed. External conviction (Lilly $2.75B deal, Sanofi, Roche, Genentech, Bayer partnerships) is far stronger than internal conviction signals.

### 3. Products / Services / Goals
- **RecursionOS** — petabyte-scale phenomics data platform; automated robotic labs; ML for drug-disease mapping
- **Exscientia integration** — generative AI chemistry design; end-to-end platform from target to clinical candidate
- **REC-4881** — Phase 1b/2 FAP/oncology; 75% polyp reduction; FDA engagement H1 2026
- **Lilly $2.75B deal** — landmark validation; multi-program collaboration (Mar 2026)
- **Sanofi, Roche, Genentech, Bayer, Merck KGaA partnerships** — milestones generating

### 7. Thesis Killer
Lilly $2.75B deal produces no approved drug within 5 years; REC-4881 stumbles on registrational pathway; platform narrative unsupported by clinical proof of concept at scale; new CEO strategic pivot away from drug discovery platform.

### 8. Investment Hypothesis
EV/Net Cash of 1.36x for a company with Eli Lilly, Sanofi, Roche, Genentech, and Bayer as paying partners is exceptional. The dataset moat (petabytes of cellular perturbation data) is irreplicable in <$10B and 10 years. Insider selling is the key negative — management is not accumulating at these levels. The thesis is: external parties (Lilly, Sanofi, Roche) are betting billions on this data platform while management is cashing out. That divergence creates risk but also the valuation opportunity — if the partnerships validate the platform, the EV/Net Cash re-rates significantly.

> **MISSION:** Decode biology with AI-scale data to find drugs faster; Lilly $2.75B deal = strongest external validation
