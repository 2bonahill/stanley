## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Manufacture zinc-based, non-flammable, long-duration battery energy storage systems (Znyth technology) at gigawatt scale to replace LFP/NMC chemistry in grid and utility applications.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO Joe Mastrangelo joined 2019 (was Board Advisor from 2018) — Track B operator, not founder. 30 years at GE: ran GE Power Conversion, President/CEO GE Gas Power Systems (15,000 employees, 60+ countries). No scientific/chemistry background. Execution risk: GE-trained executives optimise large-system businesses; Eos is a manufacturing scaling problem that requires factory-floor discipline of a very different kind. Prior CFO Nathan Kroeker became CCO Mar 2025 — CFO transition risk flagged. Multiple new hires: EVP Sales Erik Todd, SVP Projects Cristi Thomas. Class action pending against senior executives for allegedly misrepresenting manufacturing automation readiness.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
- **Eos Znyth battery**: Aqueous zinc-bromine bipolar electrode system. Non-flammable, non-toxic, degradation-tolerant (designed for 15-year life). Z3 module architecture.
- **Eos Indensity (launched Jan 2026)**: 1 GWh/acre density architecture — company claims 4x density of most competitors. Targets utility front-of-meter and large C&I projects.
- **Eos Cube**: Containerized deployments for C&I and microgrid.
- **2026 public targets**: $300–400M revenue; 2 GWh annualized production capacity achieved (exited 2025); second production line factory acceptance complete Q1 2026.

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Revenue: $300–400M FY2026 → $700M+ FY2027 (if backlog converts on schedule)
- Gross margin: Currently negative (-$143M FY2025). Must cross zero in 2026 — any sustained positive GM is a re-rating event.
- Backlog: $701.5M (2.8 GWh) at YE2025 — represents full-year coverage of 2026 guidance midpoint.
- Cash: Must be quantified; heavy burn rate ($264M FCF negative FY2025); any capital raise will dilute.

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- Q1 2026 full earnings (May 2026) — confirm $56-57M preliminary; first gross margin data point on the new production lines. Positive GM = re-rating.
- Backlog conversion rate H2 2026 — 2.8 GWh backlog at $701.5M must convert into revenue above $250M H2 to hit full-year guidance. On-schedule delivery signals manufacturing is fixed.
- Class action resolution — Yung v. Eos Energy 2:26-cv-02372. Settlement or dismissal removes overhang; adverse ruling would hit stock hard.

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current price: $6.32 (Apr 14, 2026). 52W range: $3.69–$19.86. Stock is 68% below 52W high, 71% above 52W low. Valuation: EV $3.74B / FY2026 guided revenue midpoint $350M = EV/Revenue 10.7x. For a company with negative gross margins and a class action pending, this is not cheap — it's pricing in the turnaround. GREEN band would require EV/Rev ~5–6x (implied price ~$2.80–$3.50), which is near the 52W low.

**7. THESIS KILLER**
Automated bipolar zinc manufacturing fails to achieve positive gross margin in 2026 at scale, confirming the 2025 miss was structural rather than a ramp timing issue.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
Eos is a genuine materials-science bet on zinc as an alternative battery chemistry to lithium — non-flammable, abundant, domestically manufactured, and optimised for 4–12 hour long-duration storage where LFP faces cost and safety disadvantages. The Q1 2026 $56–57M preliminary result and the new Indensity architecture suggest the manufacturing crisis is not terminal. However, the -$143M gross margin in FY2025, the pending securities class action, the 29.7% short float, and the non-founder CEO make this a high-risk speculative at current prices. The market is treating EOSE as a show-me story — it needs two consecutive quarters of positive gross margin to break the bear case.

## Technology
**Znyth (Zinc) Aqueous Battery Chemistry**
- Bipolar electrode design with aqueous zinc-bromine electrolyte — inherently non-flammable vs. LFP/NMC lithium chemistries.
- Z3 module: polymer casing, aqueous electrolyte, bipolar electrodes — designed for 15-year life with low degradation.
- 200+ patents pending, published, or issued.
- Optimised for 4–12 hour duration storage — the segment where LFP's cycle-life and flammability limitations are most acute and where FERC 841 + IRA ITC create structural policy demand.

**Eos Indensity (launched Jan 2026)**
- New platform architecture targeting 1 GWh/acre — company claims 4x density vs. most competing technologies.
- Aimed at utility front-of-meter, large-scale procurement markets.
- No confirmed commercial revenue from Indensity yet.

**Eos Cube**
- Containerized system for C&I and microgrid deployments.

**Manufacturing**
- Two production lines at Turtle Creek, PA (Pittsburgh area). Second line completed factory acceptance Q1 2026.
- Annualized capacity: 2 GWh as of YE2025 (achieved 5 weeks later than plan).
- Automated bipolar manufacturing transition caused the 2025 quality failures that triggered the class action.
