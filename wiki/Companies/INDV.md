**TEAM** — Transitioning from long-time CEO Mark Crossley to successor Joe Ciaffoni (May 2025); Ciaffoni has strong commercial experience from Bausch + Lomb but faces the challenge of managing legacy legal overhangs and scaling Sublocade.

## Part 0 — The Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
**STRONG.** Indivior is the pure-play leader in the OUD market. Their mission is central to every product they develop, focusing on the social and medical crisis of addiction.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO Mark Crossley stabilized the company post-demerger and DOJ issues. Successor Joe Ciaffoni (incoming May 2025) is a commercial specialist. Execution risk is moderate given the leadership transition.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
Core product: **Sublocade** (monthly buprenorphine injection). Goal: Reach $1B+ net revenue for Sublocade by 2026.

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Revenue: ~$1.15B (2026).
- Adj. EBITDA Margin: ~48%.
- Sublocade Market Share: Target >30% of OUD patients on LAIs.

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- Successful transition to CEO Joe Ciaffoni (May 2025).
- Q2 2026 Sublocade growth acceleration data.
- Settlement of remaining civil litigations.

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current Price: $32.52. Story 64 / Price 58. **WATCHLIST/HOLD**. Entry is compelling at $28 (retest of 200-DMA).

**7. THESIS KILLER**
Major generic erosion of Sublocade or a new, superior long-acting injectable (LAI) opioid use disorder (OUD) treatment captures the majority of the US market.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
Indivior is a "cash flow machine" priced as a legal risk. As the DOJ settlement clears and Sublocade reaches blockbuster status, the market will re-rate it from a specialty pharma laggard to a high-margin addiction platform leader.
