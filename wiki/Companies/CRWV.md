## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Build and operate the first AI-native cloud infrastructure platform, leasing GPU compute capacity at scale to AI hyperscalers, model developers, and enterprises under multi-year take-or-pay contracts.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO Michael Intrator is a co-founder with background in energy and commodities trading (HBK Investments). His commodities background maps directly to CoreWeave's model: reserve raw compute like a commodity, sell capacity forward on long contracts. Not a classic founder-technologist — but the model requires capital markets sophistication, which Intrator has demonstrated. CSO/CTO roles occupied by co-founders Brian Venturo and Brannin McBee. Execution risk: all insiders selling at pace on 10b5-1 plans post-IPO.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
CoreWeave Cloud Platform: proprietary GPU orchestration software + data center infrastructure delivering A100/H100/H200/B200 capacity. Customers reserve large blocks of GPUs under multi-year contracts (3–5 years). The model is closer to a toll road than a traditional SaaS cloud — capacity is finite, committed, and priced into the contract backlog. Key customers: Microsoft (~50%+ of recent revenue), Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic. $66B+ contracted backlog as of early 2026.

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Revenue: $5.13B (2025, +168% YoY); guidance for 2026 expected ~$8–10B
- Net loss: -$1.20B (2025); spending $2.60 for every $1 in revenue in CapEx/debt service terms
- Debt: ~$21–30B total debt at ~11% average interest rate — massive and growing
- Contracted backlog: $66B+ = extraordinary revenue visibility IF customers remain solvent and don't defect to in-house build

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- Meta $21B contract ramp (through 2032) — largest single AI infrastructure deal in history; adds predictable revenue through 2032
- Anthropic multiyear deal — validates hyperscaler-agnostic demand
- Potential margin improvement as leverage is fixed and revenue scales
- AI infrastructure CapEx cycle remaining elevated; any slowdown is an existential risk

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
IPO price $40 (March 2025). Current price ~$117 (April 2026), up ~193% from IPO. No price scoring run — mega cap exclusion applies.

**7. THESIS KILLER**
If Microsoft (currently ~50%+ of revenue) internalizes GPU compute or defects, or if OpenAI/Meta's AI spend cycle reverses, CoreWeave's $21B+ debt becomes unserviceable against collapsing revenue.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
CoreWeave has assembled the largest private GPU fleet in the world and locked it into long-term take-or-pay contracts with the most creditworthy AI spenders on earth. The bear case is that the debt load (~11% average rate, $21B+) creates fragility if any anchor customer walks, and insiders are selling aggressively post-IPO. The bull case is a toll-road model where $66B+ backlog essentially pre-funds debt service for years. Mega cap — context only.

## Technology
GPU orchestration at hyperscale. CoreWeave operates proprietary software for managing NVIDIA GPU clusters (A100, H100, H200, B200) across its own data centers. Positioned as "AI-native" vs. AWS/Azure/GCP which are CPU-first retrofitted. Differentiation: lower latency for AI workloads, faster provisioning, custom networking (InfiniBand/NVLink), and the willingness to commit to long contracts that hyperscalers won't.

## Business Model
Take-or-pay GPU reservations: customers commit to multi-year (3–5yr) contracts for GPU capacity blocks. Revenue is highly predictable (backlog $66B+) but the model requires massive upfront CapEx funded by secured debt (GPU-backed loans). Debt is collateralized by NVIDIA GPUs, which depreciate as new chip generations launch — structural risk. Main customers: Microsoft (~50%+ revenue), Meta (~$21B contract through 2032), OpenAI (multiyear), Anthropic (multiyear).
