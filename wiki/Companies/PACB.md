# Pacific Biosciences (PACB) 🟡

## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Democratize high-accuracy long-read sequencing to resolve the structural variations and complex genomic regions that short-read technology cannot reach.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO Christian Henry (ex-Illumina CCO/CFO) leads an operator-heavy team; the mission is to defend the technical "HiFi" gold standard while scaling commercial throughput.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
Core platform is **Revio** (high-throughput HiFi sequencing) and **Onso** (Short-read SBB). Public goal: Achieve positive cash flow by 2026-2027 through a shift toward recurring consumable revenue.

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- **Consumable Revenue:** $82M (FY2025) → $150M+ (2028)
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 40% (FY2025) → 55%+ (2028)
- **Cash Burn:** ~$110M annual → <$20M by YE 2027
- **Installed Base:** ~200 Revio systems → 500+ systems

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- **SPRQ-Nx Chemistry Launch (H1 2026):** Expected to drive higher utilization and lower cost-per-genome.
- **FFPE Oncology Workflow Adoption (H2 2026):** Entry into the high-margin clinical cancer research market.
- **Profitability Inflection (2027):** First quarter of neutral/positive FCF.

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current Price: ~$1.55. GREEN band entry price: <$1.40. Valuation basis: 0.55x EV/Rev-to-CAGR ratio + 59% Cash as % of Market Cap.

**7. THESIS KILLER**
Oxford Nanopore achieves <0.1% raw error rate at <$500 per genome, neutralizing the HiFi accuracy advantage and rendering PACB's hardware cost-prohibitive.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
PACB is priced for bankruptcy despite holding the gold-standard technology for long-read sequencing. The market is valuing it as a failing hardware company, ignoring the record consumable revenue (51% of mix) and structural margin expansion. With $280M cash and a 59% cash/cap ratio, the downside is protected while the Revio platform provides 10x upside if it becomes the clinical standard for complex genomics.

## Technology
