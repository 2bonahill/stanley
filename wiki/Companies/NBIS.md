## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Build the full-stack GPU cloud infrastructure layer for global AI — from bare-metal H100/H200 cluster rental to managed training, inference, and developer tools — at a cost and speed hyperscalers cannot match for specialist AI workloads.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
Founder-CEO Arkady Volozh co-founded Yandex in 1997, took it to a $1.3B IPO in 2011 (then the largest tech IPO since Google), and rebuilt from scratch post-sanctions by retaining the most technically capable Yandex engineers (~1,000+) under a Western holding structure. He renounced Russian citizenship February 2026 and owns 12.51% of shares ($4.89B at current price). CFO is Ilya Krokhin (ex-Yandex CFO). Key risk: 100% execution concentration in one founder; if Volozh departs, no clear successor of equal standing.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
Core: Nebius AI Cloud — large-scale GPU clusters (H100/H200 Blackwell); managed ML services; cloud platform for training/inference. Subsidiaries: Toloka (data annotation/labelling for GenAI), TripleTen (edtech, bootcamp model), Avride (autonomous driving spinout). Management-committed targets: $3.0–3.4B revenue FY2026; $7–9B annualized recurring revenue by EOY 2026; $16–20B CapEx in 2026; $46B total contracted backlog (Meta $27B + Microsoft $19.4B).

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Revenue: $529M FY2025 → $3.4B FY2026 (guided) → $9.7B FY2027 (analyst consensus)
- Gross margin: 68.6% FY2025 (massive jump from 37.5% in FY2024 — GPU cluster reaching operating leverage)
- EBITDA: -$141.6M FY2025 → path to breakeven FY2027 on revenue scale
- Cash/Debt: ~$6B cash vs ~$4.3B convertible notes issued March 2026 — net positive but capex-funded position
- GPU capacity: building toward multi-GW data center footprint (NJ + EU); 2026 CapEx the defining year

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- Microsoft $19.4B deal reaching full annual run-rate (FY2027 ramp) — revenue inflection confirmation
- Meta $27B deal Phase 1 delivery ($12B over 5 years starting early 2027) — binding contract execution begins
- FY2026 revenue print above $3B — validates guidance credibility for first time at this scale

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current price: ~$161.94. Kill Switch triggered (660%+ from 52W low of $20.25). 52W high is $166.10 — stock near all-time high. Not in entry zone. GREEN band would require a durable pullback to ~$80–100 (EV/Revenue 2027 of ~3–4x, still premium but justified if meta/MS ramp on schedule). Analyst targets: $129–200, average $163.

**7. THESIS KILLER**
If Meta or Microsoft terminates or materially delays their multi-billion GPU contracts, Nebius loses >80% of its guided FY2026–2027 revenue bridge before the business can diversify its customer base.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
Nebius is the only publicly traded pure-play AI cloud provider with binding $46B contracted backlog, NVIDIA preferred partner status, and a proven engineering organization — positioned as the merchant GPU cloud between the hyperscalers (AWS/Azure/GCP) and the unscalable boutique neoclouds. The market is pricing in execution risk on the Meta/Microsoft ramp; if both deals proceed on schedule, the $3.4B FY2026 and $9.7B FY2027 revenue numbers are achievable, making the current ~$41B cap look reasonable by late 2027. The bear case — 20% short float — centers on customer concentration and capital structure risk ($4.3B in convertible debt + need for ~$8B more financing for $16–20B CapEx). Not a buy at current price; monitor for meaningful pullback.
