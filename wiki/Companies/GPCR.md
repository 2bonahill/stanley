# Structure Therapeutics ($GPCR) 🟢

## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Use GPCR structural biology and biased agonism to convert injectable biologics into superior oral small molecules — starting with the $50B+ obesity/metabolic market — where structural precision enables efficacy that random-screening chemistry cannot achieve.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO/Founder Raymond Stevens, PhD. Solved the first human GPCR structure in 2007. Three prior company exits: Syrrx → Takeda (Nesina, approved T2D drug), Receptos → Celgene $7.32B (Zeposia, approved MS/UC), MemRx → Chiron/Novartis. 40+ years building the world's largest GPCR structural database. CSO Xichen Lin, CTO Yingli Ma, co-founders Mike Hanson and Jun Yoon (former Stevens lab scientists). This is the definitive founder-scientist team in the oral GPCR space. Execution risk: no open-market insider buying; CMO sold $248K.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
Lead: aleniglipron (GSBR-1290, oral GLP-1 agonist) — Phase 2b complete; 16.3% weight loss at 44 weeks (highest oral GLP-1 Phase 2 data ever); 3.4% discontinuation; Phase 3 initiation H2 2026. Oral amylin: ACCG-2671 (Phase 1 initiated Dec 2025; data H2 2026), ACCG-3535 (Phase 1 H2 2026). Muscle-sparing: ANPA-0073 (APJR agonist; Phase 1 complete, entering Phase 2). Pulmonary fibrosis: LTSE-2578 (LPA1R antagonist, Phase 1). Oral GIP and glucagon programs in research. Goal: become the oral small molecule platform for any GPCR-mediated disease.

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Phase 3 initiation for aleniglipron: H2 2026 (gated on FDA Type B meeting Q2 2026)
- Phase 1 data for ACCG-2671 (amylin): H2 2026 — validates second-franchise potential
- ANPA-0073 Phase 2 data: 2027 — validates muscle-preservation add-on
- Cash at Phase 3 initiation: ≥$1B (burn ~$225M/yr; current $1.4B = ample runway)

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- FDA Type B End-of-Phase-2 meeting Q2 2026 — gating event for Phase 3 design; positive outcome triggers Phase 3 start
- Phase 3 initiation H2 2026 — binary announcement that re-rates stock (Phase 2 data was 16.3%; Phase 3 at 180mg per confirmed Phase 2b design)
- Partnership or M&A announcement — "strategic discussions with large pharma ongoing" per Apr 12 2026 transcript; Novo/Roche/AZ/Merck identified as likely acquirers

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current price $54.51. Cash $1.4B (~$19.76/ADS). Cap $3.86B. EV ≈ $2.46B = 1.76x net cash. GREEN band: $40–$50 (EV ≤ 1.0–1.3x net cash). Valuation basis: EV/Net Cash with Phase 3 catalyst trigger.

**7. THESIS KILLER**
Phase 3 ATTR-CM-style clinical hold, major GI safety signal at Phase 3 scale, or Lilly orforglipron capturing oral GLP-1 first-mover and crowding out a second entrant at a price point that removes the commercial differentiation thesis for aleniglipron.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
Structure Therapeutics has the best oral GLP-1 Phase 2 data ever produced (16.3% weight loss, 3.4% discontinuation) and the only patent-validated structural biology platform in the oral obesity space (Roche paid $100M to access a different patent class). Ray Stevens is a 3-exit founder who built this platform over 40 years. The stock is down 42% from its 52W high despite no negative clinical events — pure risk-off rotation in GLP-1 stocks as Lilly approaches Phase 3. This is the classic "love story at the wrong time" setup before Phase 3 data.

## Technology
**GPCR Structure-Based Drug Design**: Solve the precise 3D atomic structure of a GPCR target, design small molecules to fit the binding pocket with atomic precision.

**Biased Agonism**: GLP-1 receptor signals through G-protein (desired: weight loss, insulin) and beta-arrestin (GI side effects). Aleniglipron selectively activates only G-protein — explaining the 3.4% discontinuation rate vs 5–10% for competitors.

Platform scope: aleniglipron (GLP-1), amylin receptor (ACCG-2671, ACCG-3535), APJR/apelin (ANPA-0073, muscle-sparing), LPA1R (LTSE-2578, pulmonary fibrosis), GIP receptor, glucagon receptor.
