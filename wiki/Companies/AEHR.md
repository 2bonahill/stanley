## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Design and manufacture the world's only full-wafer burn-in and test systems capable of simultaneously processing thousands of silicon carbide, GaN, silicon photonics, and AI processor chips — ensuring reliability before they reach autonomous vehicles, data centers, and grid infrastructure.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO Gayn Erickson — long-tenured leader who has guided Aehr through the SiC wave and now the AI photonics pivot. Company is founder-adjacent (Erickson has been CEO through the company's transformation). Red flag: CEO has never made an open-market purchase on record. Post-Q3 FY2026 earnings (April 9–11, 2026): COO, CTO, and multiple directors sold ~$5.6M combined within 3 days. Insider selling is a concern.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
Aehr makes two key products:
- **FOX-XP** (Full-Wafer/Multi-Die/Single-Die): The only full-wafer burn-in system that can test >10,000 chips simultaneously at production power densities. Costs $250K/wafer capacity vs. $700K–$1M for alternatives. Key advantage: eliminates infant mortality (early-life failures) before chips leave the fab.
- **WaferPak Contactors**: Consumable contact hardware enabling wafer-level testing; proprietary and high-margin.

Markets: SiC (EV powertrains) historically dominant (~40% now, down from ~90%); AI processors (Sonoma PLBI — wafer-level burn-in for AI datacenter chips) now >35%; silicon photonics (new hyperscale customer, March 2026); GaN.

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Q3 FY2026 bookings: $37.2M (record — 3.5x book-to-bill)
- Q3 FY2026 backlog: $50.9M effective
- First 5 weeks of Q4: additional $12.2M bookings
- Key metric: lead AI hyperscaler (believed to be Google/Alphabet) forecasting "substantial expansion" of Sonoma PLBI systems H2 CY2026–2027
- Revenue milestone: $100M+ quarterly run-rate if Sonoma ramp delivers

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
1. Sonoma PLBI expansion order (H2 CY2026) — lead hyperscale AI customer's "substantial expansion" forecast is the dominant near-term catalyst
2. Silicon photonics hyperscale customer production ramp (new win, March 2026) — second major AI datacenter revenue stream opening
3. SiC market recovery — automotive SiC production ramp in CY2026–2027 adds back a depressed revenue stream

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current price: ~$50 range (stock up >200% YTD 2026). Kill Switch triggered. GREEN band: $20–$28 (EV/FY2027E revenue 5–7x at $100M recovery). AMBER band: $28–$40.

**7. THESIS KILLER**
The lead AI hyperscaler (believed Google) pauses or eliminates the Sonoma PLBI procurement — by insourcing burn-in testing, adopting a competitor's solution, or shifting to an architecture with reduced burn-in requirements. This would eliminate >35% of revenue and collapse the growth thesis.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
Aehr holds a structurally singular position in semiconductor test equipment — no major competitor (Teradyne, Advantest, FormFactor) has replicated full-wafer burn-in at the power densities required by SiC, GaN, and AI chips. The 3.5x book-to-bill signals the market is accelerating. The thesis is correct — the stock has already moved 200%+ to confirm it. Wait for a meaningful pullback before entering.

## Technology
**FOX-XP System:** Full-wafer burn-in and test platform. Unlike conventional chip testers (which test chips one at a time after dicing), FOX-XP tests entire wafers simultaneously — contacting thousands of chips through proprietary WaferPak contactors. Why this matters: burn-in at operating voltage and temperature accelerates infant mortality failures before chips ship. At automotive, aerospace, and datacenter reliability standards, burn-in is mandatory. Aehr is the only commercial solution that can do full-wafer at the power densities required by SiC (automotive), AI accelerators, and GaN chips.

**Sonoma PLBI (Packaged Part Lot Burn-In):** Second product line for packaged parts post-dicing. Primary AI hyperscaler product. Enables hyperscale datacenters to burn-in entire lots of AI processors before deployment.

**WaferPak Contactors:** Proprietary consumable — enables physical contact between FOX-XP and wafer. Each customer's chip architecture requires a custom WaferPak. Creates a razor/blade recurring revenue stream once systems are deployed.
