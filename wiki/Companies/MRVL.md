## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Build the data infrastructure semiconductor layer that connects AI compute to memory, storage, and networks — custom silicon (XPUs) and optical interconnects as the spine of every hyperscaler AI cluster.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO Matt Murphy joined in 2016 as an external hire (former Maxim Integrated); not founder-led. Founders Sehat Sutardja and Weili Dai (married couple, VLSI PhD, Stanford) founded company in 1997 and were ousted in a 2016 governance crisis. Murphy has executed a decisive pivot from consumer/storage silicon to AI data center. Track record: revenue grew from ~$2.9B (FY2018) to $8.2B (FY2026). Key risk: non-founder alignment, especially with stock-based compensation dilution.

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
- **Custom AI XPUs**: Co-design and manufacture of proprietary AI accelerators for hyperscalers — Amazon (Trainium 2), Google (Axion CPU), Microsoft (Maia), Meta. Fastest-growing segment: from near-zero to $1.5B in one fiscal year.
- **Optical Interconnects**: PAM4 DSPs for 800G/1.6T, AECs, retimers, coherent DSP, co-packaged optics — the "connective tissue" between AI chips. Market view: data movement is the binding constraint, not compute speed.
- **Ethernet Switching & PHYs**: 400G/800G/1.6T switch silicon; 5G RAN chips (carrier segment).
- **Celestial AI (acquired $3.25B, 2025)**: Silicon photonics for intra-cluster optical fabrics. Management targets $500M annualized run rate by Q4 FY2028.
- **Automotive Ethernet**: Targeting $1B run rate by late 2027.

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- **1.6T optical ramp mid-2026**: First production deployments of 1.6T PAM4 DSPs — volume accelerates with hyperscaler data center buildouts.
- **2nm XPU ramp FY2028**: Second-generation custom silicon at TSMC 2nm. If custom doubles as guided, this is the next step-change in revenue.
- **Automotive Ethernet $1B milestone late 2027**: Currently small/declining; a recovery would re-rate the diversification thesis.

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current price: ~$134. 52W range: $48.09–$136.90. Stock is near all-time high and ~60% above 200-DMA — very extended. No entry thesis at current price. Analyst consensus: $150–164 (Barclays/JPM). Mega cap rule applies — price target monitoring only.

**7. THESIS KILLER**
Hyperscalers (Google, Amazon, Microsoft) in-house their XPU design capabilities and reduce reliance on Marvell, eliminating the custom silicon revenue pillar.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
Marvell has re-positioned from a diversified chip commodity supplier to the primary co-design and manufacturing partner for hyperscaler custom AI silicon — a structurally defensible role because XPU design requires Marvell's process expertise at advanced nodes. The optical interconnect super-cycle (800G → 1.6T transition) provides an independently large catalyst. Market is still pricing this as a traditional semiconductor — the architectural partner premium is not fully in consensus models.

## Technology

**Custom XPUs**: Marvell co-designs proprietary AI accelerators with hyperscalers, doing the actual silicon engineering (architecture, physical design, tape-out at TSMC N3/N2). This is "foundry-plus" — more intellectual ownership than pure manufacturing. Current 3nm programs shipping; 2nm in development with FY2028 ramp target.

**Optical Interconnects (PAM4 DSPs)**: As GPU clusters scale, bandwidth between chips becomes the bottleneck. Marvell's DSPs convert electrical to optical signals at 800G (today) and 1.6T (ramp 2026). Co-packaged optics (CPO) further integrates this into the package. Celestial AI acquisition adds silicon photonics PIC capability.

**Ethernet Switching**: Prestera and Teralynx switch silicon; competitive with Broadcom's Tomahawk line but lower market share. Growing 57% YoY in enterprise.
