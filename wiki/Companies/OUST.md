## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
Build the world's leading digital LiDAR platform — high-performance, low-cost sensing hardware combined with software intelligence — enabling machines, vehicles, and robots to perceive the physical world in 3D.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
CEO Angus Pacala — co-founder, took the company public in 2021 and led the merger with Velodyne (the original LiDAR pioneer) in 2023. The combined entity retained Pacala as CEO. Acquisition of Stereolabs (Feb 2026) was a founder-driven strategic call to add computer vision depth cameras alongside LiDAR. Founder-led = Track A. Signal: Kaizen says "great founder led company."

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
Ouster makes digital LiDAR sensors for:
- **Robotics & automation** — factory robots, warehouse AMRs, drone navigation
- **Smart infrastructure** — traffic monitoring, perimeter security, city-scale sensing
- **Automotive** — ADAS and autonomous vehicles
- **Defense/government** — surveillance and navigation

Digital LiDAR advantage: digital receiver architecture (CMOS-based) allows Ouster to produce LiDAR at dramatically lower cost than analog competitors, while increasing resolution with each silicon generation. Stereolabs acquisition (Feb 2026) adds ZED depth camera family — computer vision for robot arms and close-range 3D sensing where LiDAR is overkill.

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Revenue: watch for inflection past $100M with SAFE LiDAR Act tailwind
- SAFE LiDAR Act (June 2026): bans Chinese LiDAR (Hesai, Robosense) from US government and certain infrastructure contracts — removes two dominant low-cost competitors in the US market
- Q3 FY2026: called out by Kaizen as "big" — likely first quarter with significant SAFE LiDAR Act-driven demand
- Gross margin expansion as volume scales

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
1. SAFE LiDAR Act implementation (June 2026) — bans Chinese LiDAR in US government/infrastructure procurement; effectively removes Hesai and Robosense from Ouster's two largest US market segments
2. Stereolabs ZED X Nano commercial traction — robot arm mounting camera launched; if major robotics OEM adopts it, opens an entirely new sensing market
3. Key autonomous vehicle or infrastructure win — a Tier-1 automotive or smart city contract win would validate the full autonomous systems platform

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
Current price: below $20 per Kaizen (buying there as of recent tweets). Market cap ~$1.37B. MONITOR ONLY at current levels per agent scoring (Story 59, Price 62, both AMBER).

**7. THESIS KILLER**
The SAFE LiDAR Act is weakened, delayed, or exempted by lobbying — Chinese LiDAR remains available in US markets, removing Ouster's regulatory moat and exposing it to sustained Chinese price competition.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
Ouster is trading at trough valuation going into its most important regulatory catalyst in company history — the SAFE LiDAR Act implementation in June 2026 that locks out Chinese competitors (Hesai, Robosense) from US government and infrastructure procurement. Combined with the Stereolabs acquisition adding computer vision depth cameras, Ouster is building a full 3D sensing platform. Founder-led, digital architecture with improving cost curves, and a legislative moat incoming in Q3 FY2026.

## Technology
**Digital LiDAR:** Ouster's digital receiver architecture uses CMOS photodetectors (the same technology in smartphone cameras) rather than analog avalanche photodiodes. This enables higher integration density, better noise performance, and a roadmap tied to semiconductor scaling (cheaper + better each generation). Key product lines: OS series (general purpose), ES series (industrial/short range), DF series (street/traffic).

**Stereolabs ZED cameras:** Stereo depth cameras for close-range 3D sensing — robot arms, AGVs, indoor robotics. Ouster + Stereolabs = full range-of-sensing coverage from 0.3m (ZED) to 200m+ (OS2 LiDAR), covering every autonomous sensing need.
