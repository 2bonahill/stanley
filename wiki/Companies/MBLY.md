## Part 0 — Investment Case

### Full Investment Case

**1. MISSION**
To improve road safety and revolutionize mobility through computer vision and autonomous driving.

**2. TEAM & DRIVE**
Founder-led by Prof. Amnon Shashua. Deep technical expertise and a "safety-first" philosophy (RSS).

**3. PRODUCTS, SERVICES & GOALS**
EyeQ chips, SuperVision (Level 2++), Chauffeur (Level 3), and Drive (Level 4). Goal: Mass rollout of EyeQ6H and the first driverless VW ID. Buzz deployments in 2026.

**4. KEY METRICS IN 2–3 YEARS**
- Revenue: $3B+ (2026 guidance: ~$1.95B)
- Pipeline: $30B+ (Current: $24.5B)
- Units: 50M+ EyeQ chips annually

**5. PRICE CATALYSTS**
- SuperVision 62 launch in Porsche/Audi (H2 2026)
- Driverless L4 launch with VW (H2 2026)

**6. ENTRY PRICE ANCHOR**
$11.00 (Reference 52W Low)

**7. THESIS KILLER**
The emergence of an "end-to-end" neural network solution (like Tesla FSD) that operates on commodity hardware and eliminates the need for Mobileye's REM (mapping) and specialized chips.

**8. INVESTMENT HYPOTHESIS**
Mobileye is the "Intel of Autonomous Driving." While 2026 is a transition year with modest revenue growth, the massive pipeline ($24.5B) and the generational shift to EyeQ6H create a coiled spring. Its expansion into humanoid robotics (Mentee Robotics) provides deep-tech optionality beyond the automotive sector.
